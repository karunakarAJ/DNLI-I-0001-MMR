# DNLI-I-0001 (DNL-126) -- Clinical Safety Signal Detection Review
## Medical Monitoring Report, Data Cut 2026-02-25

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

DNL-126 (ETV:SGSH-BioM) demonstrates a safety profile dominated by infusion-related reactions (IRRs), with 100% incidence and 442 cumulative IRR events across 20 treated participants. While headline rates appear alarming (100% TEAE, 85% Grade >=3, 65% SAE), closer analysis reveals that the vast majority of SAEs are attributable to underlying MPS IIIA disease progression or intercurrent illness rather than study drug, with only 1/15 SAEs (6.7%) assessed as drug-related. No deaths, no discontinuations due to AEs, and no hepatotoxicity signals (eDISH clean) have been observed through up to 107 weeks of exposure. The principal safety signal requiring ongoing vigilance is the IRR burden -- particularly the single anaphylactic reaction in Cohort B1 and the hypoxia SAE in participant 0017-9004 -- and the high immunogenicity rate (95% ADA-positive, 60% neutralizing).

**Key IRR Observations:**

2. **IRR rates per patient-week:** A3: ~0.66 (highest, early treatment) vs A1: ~0.28 (lowest, longest exposure). IRRs appear to attenuate over time.

4. **Pre-medication effectiveness:** 75% moderate-to-severe IRR despite prophylaxis suggests current regimen is insufficient to prevent clinically significant reactions.

## RECOMMENDED ACTIONS

1. **0017-9004 Case Review:** Request comprehensive case narrative. Re-adjudicate hypoxia SAE causality given prior drug-related IRR SAE and anaphylactic reaction.

2. **Anaphylaxis Preparedness:** Update site anaphylaxis protocols; consider augmented pre-medication for B1-type (youngest) participants.

3. **ADA-IRR Correlation Analysis:** Request formal statistical analysis correlating ADA titer levels with IRR frequency and severity.

4. **Sub-threshold Liver Function Trending:** Request individual ALT/AST/TBILI scatter plots over time.

5. **0016-9006 Disease Progression Monitoring:** Review 2 unresolved SAEs (mobility decreased Day 93, dysphagia Day 119) to confirm disease progression vs. drug effect.

6. **0017-9007 Dyskinesia SAE:** Discuss at next DSMB. Dyskinesia is atypical for MPS IIIA and may warrant further characterization.

7. **Pre-medication Protocol Review:** Convene DEC to evaluate pre-medication escalation given 75% moderate-to-severe IRR despite current prophylaxis.

## OVERALL BENEFIT-RISK ASSESSMENT

**Favorable.** MPS IIIA is a fatal neurodegenerative disorder with no approved treatment. DNL-126 demonstrates no life-threatening or irreversible drug-related toxicity. The principal safety liabilities (IRRs and immunogenicity) are actively managed through pre-medication, dose modifications, and DEC oversight. Zero discontinuations across 20 participants with up to 107 weeks of exposure confirm tolerability. The benefit-risk balance supports continued dosing with enhanced monitoring for identified signals.
